Launched on December 25, 2021, the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) represents a monumental leap in our quest to understand the Universe and its origins. As the premier observatory of the next decade, JWST examines every phase of cosmic history. From the luminous glow emitted just after the Big Bang 13.5 billion years ago to the formation of galaxies, stars, and planets. Unlike its predecessor, the Hubble Space Telescope, JWST orbits the Sun at a distance of 1.5 million kilometers (1 million miles) away from Earth, stationed at Earth's second Lagrange point (L2). As JWST continues its mission, it promises to revolutionize our knowledge of the Universe, unveiling hidden secrets and inspiring generations of astronomers and scientists.
